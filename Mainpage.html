Computer programming language, any of the various languages for a digital computer to communicate a series of comprehensive instructions. Such instructions can be executed directly when expressed in a corresponding assembly language in the computer-specific numerical form known as machine language, after a simple substitution process;.

Perhaps after any "higher-level" translation. While there are many computer languages, relatively few are widely used. Machine and assembly languages are "low-level," requiring a programmer to explicitly manage all of the idiosyncratic data storage and operation features of a computer. On the other hand, high-level languages shield a programmer from worrying about such considerations, and provide a notation that is easier for programmers to write and read.
